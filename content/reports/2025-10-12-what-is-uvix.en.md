{{< char name="mew" title="Mew" >}}
<p>First, I will brief you on the objective data. UVIX is an ETF managed by Volatility Shares LLC that seeks to achieve twice the daily return of the VIX futures index using 2x leverage.</p>
<p><strong>What is the VIX (Volatility Index)?</strong></p>
<p>The VIX is a volatility index calculated by the Cboe (Chicago Board Options Exchange) that measures the expected volatility of the S&P 500 in real-time over the next 30 days. When the market is stable, the VIX remains low (around 10-15), but when the stock market crashes or faces significant uncertainty, the VIX skyrockets (30-50 or higher), earning it the nickname 'the Fear Index'.</p>
<p><strong>How UVIX Works:</strong></p>
<ul>
    <li><strong>2x Leverage Structure:</strong> If VIX futures rise by 10% in a day, UVIX theoretically aims for a 20% increase. Conversely, if the VIX falls by 10%, UVIX drops by 20%.</li>
    <li><strong>Daily Rebalancing:</strong> Due to its leveraged structure, UVIX is extremely sensitive to daily price movements and is not designed for long-term holding.</li>
    <li><strong>Based on VIX Futures:</strong> UVIX does not track the VIX index itself but rather 'short-term VIX futures'. This difference is the core reason for its massive value decay.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Recent Performance Data (Brace for impact):</strong></p>
<ul>
    <li>Over the past year, UVIX's total return was -77.07%.</li>
    <li>The average annual return since the fund's inception is -83.72%. What this means is that <strong>simply holding it causes over 80% of your assets to evaporate annually</strong>.</li>
    <li>The average monthly value decay is about 20%, leading to repeated Reverse Splits every 8 to 22 months.</li>
</ul>

{{< char name="mikael" title="Mikael" >}}
<p>Kurumi, you are once again luring novice investors to the gambling den. Human, listen to me carefully. <strong>UVIX is not an investment product. It is a time bomb.</strong></p>
<p>Did you hear her phrase, 'if you get the timing right'? Do you have any idea how absurd that is? Even the world's top hedge fund managers fail at timing the market perfectly.</p>
<p><strong>First, structural value decay is unavoidable.</strong></p>
<p>UVIX's 2x daily compounding structure amplifies short-term gains, but for long-term holding, it causes fatal value decay due to Contango and volatility drag.</p>
<p><strong>Let me explain Contango.</strong> It's when the VIX futures curve slopes upward, meaning short-term futures prices are lower than long-term futures prices. UVIX must sell expiring futures and buy more expensive long-term ones. This process inevitably incurs a loss called <strong>'Roll Cost.'</strong> The VIX futures market is in a state of contango most of the time. In other words, <strong>when the market is stable, UVIX automatically bleeds value every single day.</strong></p>
<p>Then there's <strong>leverage decay</strong>. The 2x leverage works in both directions. What happens if the VIX repeats a +10% and -10% move?</p>
<ul>
    <li>Day 1: VIX +10% → UVIX +20% (100 → 120)</li>
    <li>Day 2: VIX -10% → UVIX -20% (120 → 96)</li>
</ul>
<p>The VIX returned to its starting point, but UVIX lost 4%, going from 100 to 96! The more volatile the market, the faster this decay accelerates.</p>
<p><strong>Second, 'holding for just one night' is not realistic.</strong></p>
<p>The moments when the VIX spikes are usually when the market is in a panic. Can you calmly take profits then? Most investors, overcome by the greed of "It might go up more," hold on for another day, then another, until they've given back all their gains and end up with a loss. Without strict discipline, trying to use UVIX tactically will lead to irreversible losses.</p>
<p><strong>Third, it is unsuitable for hedging.</strong></p>
<p>UVIX only partially captures VIX spikes and fails to consistently provide 2x returns, requiring careful monitoring. If the VIX spikes and then quickly subsides, the hedging effect disappears, and you are left with losses from UVIX itself. If you want a true hedge, put options or an ETF like TAIL are far better.</p>
<p><strong>Fourth, the historical data says it all.</strong></p>
<p>Look at the number: an average annual return of -83.72% since UVIX's inception. This is not a statistical anomaly. It is a structural inevitability. Because the market is stable most of the time and VIX futures are in contango, UVIX holders are destined to lose their assets over time.</p>
