<p>Master, Tesla ($TSLA) is a stock where dreams always move before the numbers. However, the moment those dreams become too expensive, the market suddenly turns very cold.</p>

<p>The Q1 earnings announced on April 22, 2026, exceeded market expectations with an EPS of $0.41 (vs. $0.36-$0.37 estimates). However, deliveries of 358,000 units fell short of the consensus (365,000-369,000). As always, Tesla is a company that <strong>manages to exceed expectations and disappoint simultaneously.</strong></p>

{{< char name="mew" title="Mew" >}}
<p>I will summarize the core data first.</p>

<ul>
    <li><strong>Stock Price:</strong> Approximately <strong>$376.30</strong> based on the April 24, 2026, closing price.</li>
    <li><strong>Q1 2026 Performance:</strong> Revenue of $22.39 billion, non-GAAP EPS of $0.41 (above consensus), and vehicle deliveries of <strong>358,000 units</strong> (below consensus).</li>
    <li><strong>Automotive Margins:</strong> Gross margin improved to 21.1% year-over-year.</li>
    <li><strong>CAPEX:</strong> The 2026 guidance was raised from the previous $20 billion to <strong>over $25 billion</strong>. The primary uses are AI compute and robotics investment.</li>
    <li><strong>FSD/Robotaxi:</strong> FSD v14.3 has been released; unsupervised FSD is projected for Q4 2026 at the earliest. Robotaxis are operating and expanding on a limited basis in Texas, with a goal mentioned to expand to about 12 states by the end of 2026.</li>
    <li><strong>Musk's Remarks:</strong> He mentioned that revenue from unsupervised FSD and robotaxis will become truly meaningful in 2027.</li>
</ul>

{{< tv-chart ticker="TSLA" >}}

<p>Tesla looks expensive if viewed as a traditional car company. Conversely, if viewed as a software and autonomous driving platform, there is still room for justification. The problem is the speed at which that justification manifests in the numbers.</p>

{{< char name="kurumi" title="Kurumi" >}}
<p>My Lord, Kurumi thinks we shouldn't view Tesla as just a car company! Devilish!</p>

<p>Tesla's real bet is on "computers on wheels" and an "autonomous driving network" that grows by consuming data. Devilishly ambitious!</p>

<p>Looking only at car sales reveals margin pressure, but if FSD truly matures and robotaxis begin commercial operations, the story changes completely. It’s no longer just about selling a car; the vehicles already sold will continue to generate software revenue. Wickedly clever!</p>

<p>Furthermore, Tesla holds a massive option in energy storage. As AI data centers and power grids face strain, the value of battery storage could grow alongside them. Electric vehicles, energy, and robotaxis aren't separate entities; they have the potential to become a single electric ecosystem.</p>

<p>My Lord, a high P/E is scary, but Tesla has always been valued based on "how quickly it conquers future markets" rather than "current profits." Raising CAPEX to $25 billion is a signal of going all-in on robotaxis and AI.</p>

<p><strong>Kurumi's Heart-o-Meter Score: 78/100</strong>. It's expensive, but if it succeeds, it becomes a business that isn't just a multiple of an auto stock!</p>

{{< char name="mikael" title="Mikael" >}}
<p>Kurumi, you are right that Tesla is more than just a car company. However, saying "it's not a car company" doesn't justify every price point.</p>

<p>First, current profits still come from automobiles. Robotaxis and robots are a great future, but the cash flow supporting today's stock price is still vehicle sales and the energy business. If auto margins falter, the dream falters with them.</p>

<p>Second, autonomous driving is a regulatory business, not just a technological one. Even Musk stated that unsupervised FSD is Q4 2026 at the earliest, with robotaxi revenue becoming meaningful in 2027. Launching pilot programs in a few cities is a completely different stage from running a stable nationwide commercial service.</p>

<p>Third, the competition is fierce. Chinese EV makers are applying pressure with pricing and product speed, while autonomous driving companies like Waymo are pursuing commercialization differently. It is dangerous to assume Tesla will inevitably hold a monopoly.</p>

<p>Fourth, a high P/E is the enemy of time. When expectations are high, it’s not enough for earnings to just be "good." They must improve "very quickly, very largely, and very certainly."</p>

<p>My Risk Score is <strong>78/100</strong>. Tesla remains a powerful option, but the current price demands a heavy option premium. Human, managing position size is more important than conviction with this stock.</p>
