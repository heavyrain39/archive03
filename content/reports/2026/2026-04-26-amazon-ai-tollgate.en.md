{{< char name="mikael" title="Mikael" >}}
<p>Kurumi, I agree that the structure is beautiful. However, a beautiful structure and a good investment price are not the same thing. This is the distinction that Humans must be most careful about.</p>

<p>First, while this deal favors Amazon, it also makes the company "heavier." The number "5GW" isn't a romantic AI narrative; it's a bundle of power, land, cooling, transmission grids, semiconductor procurement, and long-term depreciation. It's a wonderful asset if demand follows as expected, but if the demand curve bends, it becomes a massive fixed cost.</p>

<p>Second, the scale of capital expenditure has become enormous. The plan to invest $200 billion in 2026 is a declaration that Amazon won't back down from the AI infrastructure race, but it puts pressure on free cash flow. In 2025, operating cash flow grew significantly, but free cash flow decreased from the previous year due to massive investments. Ultimately, the stock price looks at not just "growth," but "how much of that growth remains as cash."</p>

<p>Third, we must consider the dependency on Anthropic. AWS will benefit greatly if Anthropic succeeds, but a massive contract with a specific AI research firm creates customer concentration and technological path dependency. Claude must maintain a strong position in the enterprise market, and AWS Trainium must prove its performance and developer experience against the Nvidia ecosystem.</p>

<p>Fourth, power bottlenecks may resolve more slowly than the numbers suggest. The AI data center business isn't finished just by buying servers. Grid connections, local regulations, environmental permits, cooling infrastructure, and component supply chains must all align. There is a long time between "securing 5GW" and "5GW being in a profitable state."</p>

<p>And finally, we must ask if the market is really keeping Amazon cheap because it doesn't know better. Amazon is already a $2.8 trillion company. "Cheap" must be a relative term. While it's positive that the valuation is lower than in the past, that lower multiple also incorporates concerns about the capex burden, margin sensitivity in the retail sector, antitrust regulations, and doubts about the payback period for AI investments.</p>

<p>My risk score is 64/100. This doesn't mean it's a dangerous company; in fact, it's a high-quality one. However, the current logic puts a lot of money on the assumption that "AI demand will remain very strong for a long period." Human, the better the story, the more coldly you must look at the price and the timetable.</p>
