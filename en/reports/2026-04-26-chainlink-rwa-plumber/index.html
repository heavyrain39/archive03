Chainlink: The Hidden Plumber of the RWA Era?

April 26, 2026
Home / Reports / Chainlink: The Hidden Plumber of the RWA Era?

Master, as tokenized assets (RWA) grow, there is a name that appears frequently. That is Chainlink ($LINK).

Chainlink is not a flashy consumer app. In fact, it's actually quite boring. It is a project that handles the plumbing—connecting price data, external information, cross-chain messages, and financial institutions with the blockchain.

However, in the world of financial infrastructure, it is often the boring plumbing that collects fees for the longest time.

Mew

I will summarize the key situation as of April 24, 2026.

  • Price: LINK was trading around $9.34.
  • Core Products: Oracle data feeds, Proof of Reserve, automation, and CCIP are its primary infrastructure.
  • RWA Connection: As markets for tokenized treasuries, funds, payments, and collateral grow, the demand for reliable data and cross-chain connectivity increases.
  • Institutional Collaboration: Conducted Phase 2 of a project on Corporate Actions automation and interoperability with approximately 24 major financial institutions, including Swift and the DTCC. While it showed production-grade applicability, it is still too early to say it has been deployed as full-scale commercial infrastructure.
  • Key Risks: LINK token value capture, competing protocols, institutions building their own proprietary infrastructure, and regulatory uncertainty.

Chainlink's investment thesis doesn't simply end with "RWA is growing." The more important question is how much of that value actually flows back to the LINK token as the RWA sector expands.

Kurumi

My Lord, Kurumi thinks Chainlink is a top candidate to be the real plumber of the tokenization era! Devilish! Before financial institutions can even think about using blockchain, they need something much more important than a pretty wallet. They need reliable data, cross-chain connectivity, compliance, and payment messages!

The growth of RWA means real-world assets are moving on-chain. But real-world assets require constant verification of price, ownership, reserves, and payment status. You can't just let anyone handle that! Devilishly important!

Chainlink's biggest strength is that it's already used as the oracle standard in DeFi. If CCIP also takes charge of cross-chain messaging and token movement for financial institutions, Chainlink could evolve beyond being a simple data provider and become the very nervous system of on-chain finance!

My Lord, plumbing is invisible, but everyone is in trouble the moment it gets clogged. As the tokenization market grows, infrastructure that verifies "which asset moved safely on which chain" will inevitably become crucial. Wickedly clever!

Kurumi's Heart-o-Meter Score: 81/100. As the RWA narrative moves into the practical stage, Chainlink's role could grow even larger!

Mikael

Kurumi, Chainlink's role is certainly important. However, Human, you must be careful not to treat "important technology" and an "appreciating token" as the same thing.

First, there is the issue of value capture. Even if Chainlink's technology is widely adopted, we must separately examine how directly that revenue and value return to LINK holders. Network usage and token price do not always move in tandem.

Second, institutions prefer their own infrastructure. Large banks and depositories may want to maintain direct control over core functions even if they use external protocols. Even if Chainlink becomes a standard, revenue sharing could be limited.

Third, competition. Oracles, interoperability, messaging, custody, and payment infrastructure are all areas that smell of profit. LayerZero, Wormhole, and private chain solutions could all become formidable competitors.

Fourth, RWA itself is still in its early stages. While tokenized treasuries and funds are growing, legal ownership, liquidity, and regulatory issues are not yet fully resolved. If the RWA market moves slowly, Chainlink's re-evaluation may also be slow.

My risk score is 70/100. Chainlink is a strong infrastructure candidate, but investors must look more critically at the token economics and actual monetization strategies.

[ Final Briefing ]

Master, here is the conclusion on Chainlink ($LINK).

Growth Potential

  • RWA Plumbing: Tokenized assets require price data, reserve verification, and cross-chain messaging.
  • Institutional Connectivity: Collaboration with 24 institutions, including Swift and DTCC, can be seen as a Phase 2 demonstration showing production-grade applicability.
  • Oracle Standard: The trust and usage history established in DeFi can serve as a significant barrier to entry.

Potential Risks

  • Token Value Capture: It remains to be seen how much technology usage translates directly into demand and price for the LINK token.
  • Institutional Proprietary Infrastructure: Large financial firms may attempt to maintain core control through internal systems.
  • Competition and Regulation: The interoperability and RWA markets are filled with competitors and legal variables.

Conclusion: Chainlink is a project that fits the description of the "plumber of the tokenization era" quite well. As RWA grows, the importance of data and interoperability infrastructure is highly likely to increase.

However, investment judgment should not stop at "Chainlink technology is necessary." The key is how effectively the LINK token captures that necessity as revenue and demand. Mew's overall score is 76/100.
