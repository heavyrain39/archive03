Coherent ($COHR) Just Plummeted. Is This a Buying Opportunity?

August 16, 2025

Master, you've brought a very interesting topic. Just yesterday, Coherent ($COHR), which had been in the market spotlight as Druckenmiller's new favorite, saw its stock price plummet by 16% after its earnings announcement.

For you, Master, who might be confused between the 'master's choice' and the 'market's harsh verdict,' the three of us will delve deep to determine if this sudden plunge is a fall into despair or a golden buying opportunity.

Mew

Master, I will begin the briefing. Coherent's stock plunge was due to its Q4 FY2025 (ending June 30) earnings report, released after the market closed on August 13, and more importantly, its future outlook (guidance).

  • The earnings themselves weren't bad: Q4 revenue was $1.53 billion and earnings per share (EPS) were $1.00, beating Wall Street estimates on both counts. Notably, driven by demand from AI data centers, the Networking division's revenue surged by 39% year-over-year.

  • The problem was the guidance (outlook): The company projected next quarter's revenue to be in the range of $1.46 billion to $1.60 billion, a disappointing figure that fell below Wall Street's consensus.

  • The cause wasn't 'AI': Management explained that while demand from AI data centers and the communications sector remains strong, the slump in its other two pillars, the Materials and industrial Lasers divisions, held it back. The combined revenue of these two divisions decreased by 8% in the second quarter. In other words, the AI growth engine is running at full steam, but the performance of other traditional business units is dragging down overall growth.

In conclusion, it appears the market reacted more strongly to the 'slump in traditional industries' than to the 'growth potential of AI,' leading to a sell-off.

Mikael

Human, before you call this an 'opportunity,' you must first consider the possibility that it's a 'trap.' An earnings shock, especially a 'guidance shock' that presents a bleak future outlook, is one of the most dangerous signals for a stock price decline.

First, the company has officially admitted, "Our performance will be worse than expected." The underperformance of the company's other business segments (Materials, Lasers), which was hidden behind the flashy wrapper of AI, has now surfaced, and it's more severe than anticipated. This could mean that the AI engine alone is not enough to pull the entire massive ship forward.

Second, you must remember that Druckenmiller's purchase is 'past information.' He bought a large stake in Coherent before June 30. Could he have known about this disappointing guidance in advance? Unlikely. He might even be re-evaluating his own judgment right now. Catching a falling knife just because 'a master investor bought it' is an extremely risky move.

Third, market confidence has been shattered. A company that issues disappointing guidance once is likely to offer a conservative outlook in the next quarter as well. With lowered investor expectations, it becomes difficult for the stock price to rally strongly as it did before. It could take a significant amount of time to regain lost trust.

🚨 Mikael's Risk Score: 85/100

No matter how compelling the story, if it cannot be proven with numbers, it is nothing but a mirage. You should approach this conservatively until the fog of uncertainty, sown by the company itself, has cleared.

Kurumi

Mika-pi is just trying to scare you again! My Lord, this is a heaven-sent bargain sale! The real opportunities to get rich come when everyone is trembling in fear, you know!

First, the most important growth engine is perfectly fine! Look at the earnings report again! It said demand from AI data centers and the communications sector is 'strong'! The Networking division grew by a whopping 39%! The fact that it's a key beneficiary of the AI revolution, the biggest trend changing the world right now, remains unchanged. The slump caused by short-term industrial cycles is a problem that will resolve over time, but the massive wave of AI is just beginning! The reason giants like Druckenmiller invested in this company must have been for this long-term picture!

Second, the market is panicking and throwing away jewels for pennies! The stock market is like a child; if the candy right in front of its eyes (this quarter's earnings) is smaller than expected, it cries without even thinking about the giant cake it will get in the future (AI growth potential). A blue-chip stock with an intact long-term growth story plummeting due to such short-term issues—isn't this exactly what grandpa Warren Buffett called 'the best opportunity created by market volatility'?

The courage to buy what others are throwing away cheaply as they flee in terror! That is the very source of great returns! Devilish!

💖 Kurumi's Heart-o-Meter Score: 95/100

Great fortune doesn't come to those who avoid a short-term shower, but to those who get soaked while waiting for the rainbow!

〔 Final Briefing 〕

Master, I will summarize the perspectives of the three of us.

Growth Potential (Kurumi)

  • AI Growth Story Remains Valid: The cause of the earnings dip is merely a temporary slump in traditional divisions; the core growth engine, the AI data center and networking segment, is still growing strong.
  • Attractive Price Due to Overselling: The market overreacted to the short-term negative guidance, causing the stock to plummet, which presents an opportunity to buy shares at a very attractive price from a long-term perspective!
  • The Vision of a Master Investor: An investor like Druckenmiller wouldn't have invested based on short-term results alone. His investment was likely a bet on the company's long-term technological strength and its key role in the AI era!

Potential Risk (Mikael)

  • Severity of the Guidance Shock: The company's direct expression of a lack of confidence in future performance is a clear warning sign that seriously damages investor sentiment.
  • Valuation Trap: With the underperformance of other divisions, previously masked by AI hype, now confirmed, the company's overall valuation may need to be reassessed. The stock might not be cheap; it could just be finding its proper value.
  • Risk of a 'Falling Knife': While a technical rebound may occur after a sharp drop, the possibility of further decline cannot be ruled out if fundamental concerns about performance are not resolved.

Key Data (Mew)

  • The Issue: Q4 FY2025 earnings beat expectations, but guidance for the next quarter fell short, causing a 16% stock price drop.
  • Positive Factor: The Networking division (related to AI, communications) showed robust strength with 39% year-over-year growth.
  • Negative Factor: Combined revenue from the Materials and Lasers divisions (related to traditional industries) decreased by 8%, dragging down overall performance.

Master, Coherent is currently in a state where two massive forces are colliding head-on: the 'long-term AI growth story' and the 'short-term slump in its traditional industries.' As Mikael warned, the uncertainty revealed by the company itself is a risk signal that cannot be taken lightly. However, as Kurumi argued, the market's excessive fear could also be an unparalleled opportunity for a contrarian investor.

Ultimately, the success of this investment will depend on your analysis, Master, to determine whether this performance dip is merely a temporary 'growing pain' or the beginning of a 'crack' in the company's fundamental growth story.
