Privacy Policy

Effective Date: June 27, 2025

Welcome to Archive 03 (the "Blog"). Your privacy is important to us. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect your information when you visit our website.

1. Information We Collect

Our Blog is designed to be accessible without requiring user registration. However, some information may be collected through third-party services to enhance your experience:

  1. Comments via giscus:
    • Information Collected: When you choose to leave a comment, you will be prompted to log in with your GitHub account. Your GitHub profile information (such as your username and profile picture) will be publicly displayed next to your comment via the giscus service.
    • How It's Collected: We do not directly store or process this information on our servers. All authentication and data handling for comments are managed by GitHub and giscus according to their respective privacy policies.
  2. Automatically Collected Information:
    • Cookies: We may in the future use services like Google Analytics or Google AdSense, which use cookies to function. A cookie is a small piece of data sent to your browser. It does not contain personally identifiable information. You can control or refuse cookies through your web browser's settings.
    • Server Logs: Our hosting provider, GitHub Pages, may automatically log information such as your IP address, browser type, and visit times. This data is used for statistical analysis and to ensure the security and stability of our service.

2. How We Use Your Information

The information collected is used for the following purposes:

  • To enable interactive features, such as the comment section.
  • To understand website traffic and usage patterns, which helps us improve our content and user experience.
  • To potentially serve relevant advertising in the future (if implemented).

3. Data Retention

We do not store your personal data on our servers. Comments posted via giscus will remain visible on the Blog as long as the Blog is online, unless you choose to delete them through your GitHub account or delete your GitHub account itself.

4. Your Rights and Choices

You have control over your information:

  • Comments: You can edit or delete your own comments at any time by logging into GitHub.
  • Cookies: You can configure your web browser to refuse all cookies or to indicate when a cookie is being sent.
